Beshbarmak

  • Time: 3 hours 30 minutes
  • Serves: 3 hours
Ingredients Quantity
Lamb 1.5 kg
Water for broth 2.5 L
Carrot 100 g
Onion 3 pcs (400 g)
Dough 800 g

Step 1: Place the meat, 1 peeled onion, and carrot in a pot. Add 2 liters of water.

Step 2: Bring to a boil and remove the foam. Cover and simmer the broth for 2–3 hours on low heat.

Step 3: Sift the flour, add 1 egg, 1 tsp salt, 200 ml water, and knead a firm dough. Knead for at least 10 minutes. The dough should not stick to your hands.

Step 4: Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Step 5: Divide the dough into 3 parts.

Step 6: Roll out each part into a thin sheet, about 1–1.5 mm thick.

Step 7: Cut the dough into rectangles or diamonds about 5–7 cm. Leave to dry for 20 minutes.

Step 8: Remove the onion, carrot, and meat from the broth. Separate the meat from the bones and cut into small pieces.

Step 9: Slice 2 onions into half-rings and pour broth over them so that the onions are fully covered.

Step 10: Bring to a boil, add salt and pepper to taste. Simmer for 2 minutes on low heat, then remove from the stove.

Step 11: Dilute 0.5 liters of broth with water (1.5 L total) and bring to a boil — this broth will be used to cook the cut dough.

Step 12: Add 1 tbsp salt and ground pepper to taste.

Step 13: Boil the rectangles or diamonds of dough in the boiling broth in 2–3 batches for about 2 minutes each.

Step 14: Remove the cooked pieces with a slotted spoon and place them on a large plate.

Step 15: Cook the remaining dough and place it on the plate as well.

Step 16: Top the dough with pieces of meat, and place the sautéed onions on top of the meat.

Step 17: Sprinkle the finished dish with green onions and serve the broth separately.

Baursaq

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 12 hours
Ingredients Quantity
Flour 350 g
Dry yeast 5 g
Sugar 1 tsp
Salt 0.5 tsp
Milk 200 ml

Step 1: Add salt and vegetable oil to the sponge.

Step 2: Sift the flour and knead the dough.

Step 3: The dough should be soft and not sticky.

Step 4: Leave the yeast dough in a warm place for 60 minutes.

Step 5: Punch down the dough.

Step 6: Cover with a bowl and let it rest for 5 minutes.

Step 7: Roll out the dough to about 1 cm thick. Cut into circles, diamonds, or squares.

Step 8: Cover and let the dough rise for 10 minutes.

Step 9: Heat enough vegetable oil so that the baursaks can float in it. Fry the baursaks on all sides in heated (but not smoking) oil until golden brown.

Kuurdak

  • Time: 1 hour
  • Serves: 4 hours
Ingredient Amount
Potatoes 600 g
Meat offal (lung, heart, or liver) 400 g
Fat (kurdyuk fat) 400 g
Onion 160 g
Water (or broth) 200 ml
Ground black pepper a pinch (to taste)
Salt 0.5 tbsp (to taste)

Step 1: Peel, wash, and slice the onion into half-rings or cubes.

Step 2: Cut the kurdyuk fat into small cubes.

Step 3: Wash the offal (lung, liver, or heart) and cut into thin strips or small pieces.

Step 4: Peel, wash, and cut the potatoes into small wedges.

Step 5: Fry the chopped onion and the offal (lung, liver, or heart) with the kurdyuk fat. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, for about 15–20 minutes.

Step 6: Add the potato pieces.

Step 7: Add some broth or water (about 200 ml), salt, and pepper, and simmer the kuyrdak with potatoes over low heat, covered, until cooked (about 30 minutes).

Step 8: Kazakh kuyrdak is ready. Serve hot. Enjoy your meal!
